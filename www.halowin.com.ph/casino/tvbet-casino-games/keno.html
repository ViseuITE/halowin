Difference between Keno Games and Bingo

In keno, players have the freedom to select specific numbers to wager on, while in Bingo, players receive a card with a pre-determined set of numbers. Unlike Bingo, where no bets are placed, in keno, players must actively choose their numbers and consider the game's odds.

Although both games involve numbered cards and draws, keno requires more player involvement and decision-making. Newcomers to keno may initially perceive it as akin to bingo; however, keno strategy shares more similarities with selecting lottery numbers than with bingo. In essence, bingo and keno are almost diametrically opposed to each other in terms of gameplay and strategy.
