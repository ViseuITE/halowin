Phoenix Fuel Masters Advance, Beating Meralco Bolts!

This blog is all about looking back at the recent Phoenix vs Meralco basketball game in the Commissioner Cup 2023-24. We'll discuss what happened during the match, the scores of the players, the game stats, and the past record of these teams going head-to-head.

Quick Summary of Phoenix vs Meralco Match

Phoenix has secured its first "twice-to-beat" advantage since the 2020 Philippine Cup by winning seven out of the last eight games. Phoenix secured a valuable "twice-to-beat" advantage for the PBA Commissioner’s Cup quarterfinals by winning 93-83 against Meralco at the Araneta Coliseum on Wednesday, January 10.

Jason Perkins led the way, and import Johnathan Williams III had a great overall performance as the Fuel Masters won and now hold the second spot with an 8-2 record. The top spot is held by Magnolia with a 9-2 record.

Detailed Review on Phoenix Fuel Masters vs Meralco Bolts Score

Perkins scored the most in the game with 22 points and grabbed 8 rebounds. Williams also did well with 19 points, 15 rebounds, and 5 assists. This victory earned Phoenix its first "win-once" incentive since the 2020 Philippine Cup. Even if Phoenix ends up tied with San Miguel (7-2), Barangay Ginebra (7-2), and Meralco (7-3) when the elimination round concludes, they will secure no lower than the fourth-place position.

The Bolts were down by 14 points in the middle of the fourth quarter, 72-86 as per the Phoenix Fuel Masters vs Meralco Bolts Scorecard. However, they tried to make a comeback and got close at 83-88 with an 11-2 run. This run was started and finished by a Cliff Hodge three-pointer and a slam dunk from Shonn Miller.

Phoenix faced some trouble with two turnovers, but Meralco couldn't take advantage. Instead, Fuel Masters' captain RJ Jazul made a crucial three-point shot with less than 1 minute and 20 seconds left, which was a significant blow to Meralco.

Jazul scored 13 points, grabbed 5 rebounds, and gave 3 assists, while Tyler Tio contributed 15 points. This helped Phoenix win seven out of its last eight games.

Perkins and Tio scored 8 and 7 points, respectively, in the first quarter. During this time, the Fuel Masters had their largest lead of the game, leading 31-16.

Miller scored 19 points, grabbed 9 rebounds, and made 5 steals in the game for the Bolts. Unfortunately, they couldn't continue the momentum from their previous win against the league-leading Hotshots in Iloilo City last Saturday.

For Meralco to reach the top four and earn a playoff bonus, they must win their last game against Terrafirma on Friday, January 12. Additionally, either San Miguel or Ginebra must lose their final game.

Chris Banchero added 15 points, 4 rebounds, and 4 assists for the Bolts, while Chris Newsome contributed 13 points, 7 assists, 5 rebounds, and 3 steals.
